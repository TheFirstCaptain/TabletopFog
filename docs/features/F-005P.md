# F-005P: Campaign Encounter Gallery Simplification

## Objective

Simplify the Campaign page into a full encounter gallery and management page by
removing the embedded Encounter Workspace area and removing the separate Manage
Encounters mode.

## User Value

The GM can open a campaign and immediately see the prepared encounters without
the page being dominated by an unused workspace placeholder. Adding and managing
encounters happens directly on the Campaign page, while opening an encounter
takes the GM to the focused Encounter Workspace page.

## Non-Goals

- Do not change the Campaign Library page.
- Do not remove the Encounter Workspace page.
- Do not add fog controls.
- Do not change Player Display behavior.
- Do not change storage, API routes, campaign metadata, or map asset handling
  unless the implementation review finds a small compatibility cleanup is
  required.
- Do not change the selected/editing versus shown-to-players model.
- Do not add new VTT concepts such as tokens, notes, characters, initiative, or
  dynamic lighting.
- Do not add animations or transitions.

## Acceptance Notes

- The Campaign page is dominated by encounter cards, not an Encounter Workspace
  panel or placeholder.
- The embedded Encounter Workspace area is removed from the Campaign page.
- Clicking or otherwise opening an encounter navigates to the Encounter
  Workspace page for that encounter.
- Opening an encounter still does not change the Player Display.
- `Show to Players` remains the only action that updates the Player Display.
- The `Manage Encounters` button and separate Manage Mode are removed.
- The Add Encounter upload control is available directly on the Campaign page
  without first entering Manage Mode.
- Existing encounter management actions remain available from the Campaign page
  without a mode switch, including rename, reorder, and delete where those
  actions are currently supported.
- Encounter management controls are visually quiet enough that the primary
  gallery task remains clear.
- Deleting an encounter remains confirmed and remains blocked for encounters
  currently `Shown to Players`.
- Selected/prep state remains visible enough to support returning from an
  Encounter Workspace, but it must not dominate the Campaign page.
- The current `Shown to Players` encounter remains visually distinguished.
- Narrow viewport behavior remains usable without horizontal overflow.
- No Player Display, storage, API, fog, campaign-library, or VTT-scope changes
  are introduced.
