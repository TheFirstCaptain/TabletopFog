# F-008E: Drag-To-Size Circle Fog Shape

## Objective

Add a true `Circle` fog shape that works like `Rectangle`: the GM presses on
the map, drags to choose the circle size, previews the shape during the drag,
and releases to commit one circular hide or reveal operation.

## User Value

The GM can quickly cover round rooms, towers, cave chambers, spell areas, and
other circular map regions without estimating a fixed brush size first.

## Non-Goals

- Do not replace Rectangle.
- Do not remove Brush.
- Do not add polygon, lasso, snap, measurement, dynamic lighting, tokens, line
  of sight, or automation.
- Do not change Player Display read-only behavior.
- Do not change which encounter is `Shown to Players`.
- Do not migrate storage/API terminology such as `maps`, `activeMapId`, or
  `fog`.

## Acceptance Notes

- Fog tools include `Rectangle`, `Brush`, and `Circle` as separate choices.
- `Rectangle` remains drag-to-size rectangular fog.
- `Brush` remains fixed-size freehand circular painting.
- `Circle` is drag-to-size and commits one circular operation per completed
  drag.
- Hide and Reveal apply to the active tool.
- The Circle preview is visible during drag and uses the same hide/reveal
  visual treatment as other drafts.
- Tiny drags do not create fog operations.
- Escape during an in-progress circle drag cancels the draft without changing
  fog.
- The committed circle uses normalized map-relative center coordinates and a
  radius normalized against the shorter map side.
- Circle operations persist as existing ordered `hide-circle` and
  `reveal-circle` operations.
- Circle changes respect selected/editing versus `Shown to Players`
  boundaries.
- Player Display remains read-only.
